About Me


Hi I’m Tristan O’Hanlon.

I am a registered and practising senior Physics teacher in Auckland. I have been teaching in New Zealand schools for 10 years, specialising in all levels of NCEA and Cambridge International Examinations (CIE) Physics curricula as well as NZQA Scholarship Physics which many of my students have achieved.

My pass rates are exceptional and I have had students go on to study Engineering, Physics and Medicine at universities such as Oxford, Stanford and Monash.

I am on the organising committee of the NZ Young Physicists Tournament and coordinate the Auckland CERN Masterclass at the University of Auckland. I have also run overseas school Physics trips to Europe and the USA, visiting sites such as CERN and NASA.

Physics can be a difficult subject for some and I am committed to helping each of my students achieve to the best of their ability and at the highest possible level.

Extracurricular Involvement

International Young Physicists Tournament (IYPT)

This is a worldwide tournament with the NZ competition taking place each March. I have coached several high-performance teams. The most recent result was 2017: 1st in Auckland, 4th in NZ. I have coached 3 New Zealand representatives and am currently on the national organising committee.

CERN Masterclass

This is a worldwide event held annually at the University of Auckland. Along with Dr David Krofcheck, I give a talk on Particle Physics and how to analyse collision results in the CMS detector (part of my University study). Students analyse actual data from the Large Hadron Collider.

University of Auckland Engineering Science Competition
