About Me


Hi, I’m Tristan O’Hanlon.

I am a registered senior Physics teacher and Professional Teaching Fellow at the University of Auckland with over 15 years of experience in education. My career spans teaching NCEA and Cambridge International Examinations (CIE) Physics in New Zealand schools, coordinating high-achieving departments, and lecturing undergraduate physics and teacher education at university level.

My students have achieved exceptional results, including NZQA Scholarships and placements at top universities like Oxford, Stanford, and Monash. I’ve been honored with the Rutherford Trophy (2024) from the NZ Institute of Physics and the Faculty of Science Dean’s Award for Sustained Teaching Excellence (2022).

I am passionate about making physics accessible and engaging, using hands-on demonstrations and real-world context. I also contribute to the field through leadership in initiatives like the NZ Young Physicists Tournament, CERN Masterclass, and the Auckland Programme for Space Systems.
