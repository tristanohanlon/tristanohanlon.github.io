About Me

I’m Tristan O’Hanlon, a dedicated educator with over 15 years of experience in physics education, spanning secondary schools to university lecturing. As a registered teacher (Registration Number: 310158) and Professional Teaching Fellow at the University of Auckland, I’ve built a career on fostering student success, leading innovative programmes, and advancing physics education.

My teaching philosophy prioritises building relationships, reflective practice, high expectations, hands-on learning, and contextual demonstrations. This approach has yielded exceptional student outcomes—my students have earned numerous NZQA Scholarships, and many have progressed to prestigious universities such as Oxford, Stanford, and Monash. At the University of Auckland, I’ve lectured undergraduate physics, trained future teachers, and coordinated groundbreaking projects like the Auckland Programme for Space Systems (APSS), which launched New Zealand’s first student-built satellite, APSS-1, in 2020.

My contributions have been recognised with the Rutherford Trophy (2024) from the NZ Institute of Physics and the Faculty of Science Dean’s Award for Sustained Teaching Excellence (2022). I’m deeply involved in the physics community, organsing annual professional development opportunities and coordinating the Auckland CERN Masterclass.
